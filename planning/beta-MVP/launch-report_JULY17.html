§ 06 — TestFlight for a PWARead this before Sunday.

Blunt truth

TestFlight is Apple's beta channel for native iOS apps built with Xcode. You have a React + TypeScript + Supabase PWA. There is no .ipa to upload. TestFlight rejects web wrappers unless they're packaged as a real iOS binary. Do not spend Saturday trying to submit one.

Your three real options, ranked

Option A — Ship as a PWA (do this Sunday)

  • Send Founding Members a direct URL (Vercel or your custom domain). No app-store review, no queue, no fees.
  • Include a 1-screen install card — one screenshot each for iOS Safari (Share → Add to Home Screen) and Android Chrome (menu → Install app).
  • After install, the PWA behaves like a native app: full-screen, offline shell, home-screen icon, splash color from manifest.
  • Effort: 2h. Updates ship on every git push in ~30 seconds — a real advantage over the App Store.

Option B — Wrap in Capacitor, then TestFlight (do this in August, not this week)

  • Capacitor treats your existing React app as a WebView inside a native iOS shell.
  • Steps: npm i @capacitor/core @capacitor/ios · npx cap init · npx cap add ios · npx cap sync · open Xcode · build archive · upload to App Store Connect · distribute via TestFlight.
  • Requires: Apple Developer account ($99/yr), a Mac with Xcode, and 1–2 weeks first-time setup (provisioning, entitlements, icon sets, screenshots).
  • TestFlight caps: 100 internal testers (immediate, no review); 10,000 external testers (Apple beta review, 24–48h).
  • Storage/push/deep-linking that live on window.claude-style native APIs would be re-wired through Capacitor plugins — worth an afternoon of scoping before committing.

Option C — Native rewrite

No.

PWA launch setup for Sunday — concrete steps

  1. Confirm manifest + icons + service worker are live on your production domain (they are — PRs #132/#134).
  2. Test "Add to Home Screen" on your own iPhone Safari and one Android device, end-to-end.
  3. Write the install card: 2 screenshots + 3 lines of copy. Show it on first visit if !window.matchMedia('(display-mode: standalone)').matches.
  4. In the launch email, lead with the install instruction, not "click here to sign up."
  5. Feedback: in-app widget + a plain email (feedback@nothingtowear.app or a Notion form).
  6. Warn expectations: no push notifications on iOS PWA below 16.4; even above, limited. Say so up front.

§ 13 — Blind SpotsWhat a co-founder would catch that you'd miss.

Terms of Service. You need one, even a 1-pager. Same URL structure as privacy. Free templates fine.
Google Limited-Use disclosure. Verbatim language required for Gmail scopes. Copy from Google's policy page.
Account deletion end-to-end test. Built, but drive it against a real account this week.
Data export human-readable? Users will ask. Ship both CSV and JSON if you can.
Screen-reader pass. VoiceOver on closet grid + Gmail Import flow. 15 min.
Email deliverability. Launch from what domain? SPF/DKIM/DMARC set? Test to Gmail, Outlook, iCloud.
Cookie / analytics consent banner. PostHog + Sentry technically require one in EU. Minimal banner or geo-restrict.
Rate limiting on OAuth callback & signup. Protect against a bored beta user hammering endpoints.
7-day test-mode refresh token. Onboarding copy or users silently disconnect Gmail after a week.
Supabase Storage backups. Not just Postgres. Confirm bucket versioning for images.
Support inbox. One dedicated address you actually watch — not your personal Gmail.
"Delete my account" is discoverable. In Settings, not buried. Trust signal.
iOS PWA quirks. No push below iOS 16.4, limited above. Manage expectations.
Zero-results Gmail import. Empty state that isn't "you failed."
Vercel or Supabase outage Sunday? Status page (single line "we're checking") on a static host.
Legal entity behind the app. Required in privacy policy. Sole prop or LLC name.
Mobile nav choice. Your proposal Closet · Care · Search · Email · Add is close — swap to Closet · Care · [Add-FAB] · Search · Email to match the shipped BottomNav; FAB centered is a thumb-reach win.
You are the single point of failure for 30 people. Block calendar. Tell family.
